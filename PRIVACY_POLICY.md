# WeekUp Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** May 16, 2026  
**Last updated:** May 16, 2026

---

> **Summary.** WeekUp is a habit-accountability app. You sign in with your phone number, commit to short habit sprints, and post **photo proof** each day. Much of what you do is **visible to other users** by design (feed, leaderboard, public profiles). We store your data on **Supabase** (database + file storage), send **SMS OTPs** for login, deliver **push notifications** via **Expo**, and—if you subscribe—process payments through **Razorpay**. We do **not** sell your personal data or run third-party advertising analytics in the app today.

## 3. Information we collect

We collect information you provide, information generated by your use of the app, and a small amount of technical data needed to run the service.

### 3.1 Account and identity

| Data | Source | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Phone number** (E.164 format) | You, at sign-up | Authentication via one-time password (OTP); account recovery; optional friend matching |
| **User ID** (UUID) | Generated by our auth provider | Ties your profile, habits, and check-ins to your account |
| **Username** | You choose at onboarding | Public identifier (`@username`); login is **not** username-based |
| **Session tokens** | Auth provider | Keeps you signed in; stored locally on your device |

We do **not** require an email address to use WeekUp today.

### 3.2 Profile and preferences

| Data | When collected | Notes |
|------|----------------|-------|
| **Avatar** | Optional upload | Stored in cloud storage; visible to other users |
| **Bio, cover image, theme** | Pro / profile settings (if enabled) | Optional; visibility depends on your settings |
| **`is_public` profile flag** | Account settings | If your profile is not public, you are excluded from global leaderboards |
| **Notification preference** | Onboarding / settings | Whether you opted in to push reminders |

### 3.3 Habits, check-ins, and streaks

| Data | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Habit title and description** | Shown on your profile and, if the habit is public, in social surfaces |
| **Sprint dates, duration, visibility** (`public` / `private`) | Drives streak logic and who can see activity |
| **Daily check-in** (date, caption) | One check-in per habit per calendar day (IST) |
| **Proof photo** | **Required** for a normal check-in; uploaded to our storage |
| **Streak counts** (`current_streak`, `longest_streak`, `last_check_in`) | Used in the app, feed badges, and leaderboards |
| **Freeze / lifecycle events** | Records when you use a freeze token, abandon, or restart a habit (where applicable) |

**Important:** Check-ins are designed to be **permanent**. The app does not let you edit or delete a submitted check-in. This is intentional for accountability.

### 3.4 Social and community

| Data | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Follow relationships** | Who you follow and who follows you |
| **Claps** | Lightweight reactions on check-ins |
| **Nudges** | Short messages you send to another user (rate-limited) |
| **Blocks** | Users you have blocked (stored so we can hide them from your feed, leaderboard, and interactions) |
| **Reports** | If you report content or a profile, we store your report reason and target reference for moderation |

### 3.5 Contacts (optional)

If you grant **Contacts** permission:

1. The app reads phone numbers (and display names) **on your device**.
2. Numbers are normalized (e.g. Indian `+91` formatting) and sent to our server in a batch (up to **200** numbers per request) to find which contacts already use WeekUp.
3. We **do not** upload or permanently store your full address book. Only numbers you send in that request are used for matching; unmatched entries are kept on your device for invite UI only.

Contact matching compares numbers against **phone numbers stored in our authentication system** for existing users.

### 3.6 Device and technical data

| Data | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Expo push token** | Stored in a dedicated table, readable only by you and our server jobs—not by other users |
| **App logs in development** | Console warnings/errors on your device during development; we do not operate a separate crash-analytics SDK (e.g. Sentry) in the current app build |
| **OTP rate-limit metadata** | Phone number and approximate IP on OTP requests, to prevent abuse |
| **Payment webhook payloads** | Stored as payment events when you use Razorpay (subscription status, plan, Razorpay IDs) |

### 3.7 Local storage on your device

We use **AsyncStorage** on your phone to cache:

- Auth session (via the Supabase client)
- Profile and subscription summary
- Habit/check-in cache for faster load
- Short-lived pending phone number during OTP flow

You can clear this by signing out or uninstalling the app. Server-side data remains until you delete your account.

### 3.8 Payments (WeekUp Pro)

If you subscribe:

| Data | Processor |
|------|-----------|
| Subscription plan, status, billing period | **Razorpay** + our database |
| Razorpay customer / subscription IDs | Our database (not full card numbers—we never receive your full payment card details) |

Card and UPI details are entered on **Razorpay’s** hosted flows, not inside WeekUp’s UI.

### 3.9 Content moderation (text only)

Before certain text is saved (habit titles, check-in captions), we may send the text to our **`moderate-text`** server function, which calls **OpenAI’s Moderation API**. We log a short **audit record** (snippet, surface, flagged yes/no). We do **not** send your proof photos to OpenAI for moderation in the current implementation.

If moderation is unavailable, the app may **fail open** (allow the text) and still log the attempt—see [Section 9](#9-security).

## 5. What other people can see

WeekUp is built around **social accountability**. Please read this section carefully.

### 5.1 Generally public

Depending on your settings, other authenticated users may see:

- Your **username**, **avatar**, and (if set) **bio / cover**
- **Public habits** and their titles
- **Check-ins** on public habits—including **proof photos** and captions—subject to access rules below
- Your **streak counts** on leaderboards and profiles (if your profile is public)
- **Claps** and activity timestamps on shared content

### 5.2 Followers and “today” proofs

- The **feed** shows recent check-ins from people you **follow**, for **public** habits only.
- **Proof photos** live in a **private storage bucket**. Access is enforced server-side: you can always see your own proofs; others may receive **short-lived signed URLs** only when policy allows (for example, **today’s** proof in IST for a public habit from someone you follow or on a public profile).

### 5.3 Private habits (Pro)

If you mark a habit **private**:

- Other users **cannot** see that habit or its check-ins in the feed or on your public profile.
- You may still appear on leaderboards only if your **profile** remains public (leaderboard entries reflect public activity).

### 5.4 Leaderboard

Global leaderboards include users whose **profile is public** and who have qualifying public streak activity. Private profiles are excluded from those rankings.

### 5.5 What we do not expose to other users

- Your **phone number** (other users see username only)
- Your **push notification token**
- Your **payment card or bank details**
- **Private habits** and their check-ins (except to you)
- Users you **block** (and who blocked you) are filtered from each other’s social surfaces

## 8. Data retention

| Category | Retention |
|----------|-----------|
| **Account data** | Until you delete your account, then removed as described below |
| **Check-ins and proof photos** | Retained while your account exists; check-ins are not user-deletable by design |
| **Push token** | Updated while you use the app; removed on account deletion |
| **OTP rate-limit logs** | Kept for a limited period for abuse prevention |
| **Moderation audit logs** | Kept for moderation and safety review |
| **Payment / subscription records** | Kept as required for tax, accounting, and fraud prevention |
| **Reports** | Kept until resolved and as needed for legal/safety records |

### Account deletion

You can delete your account in the app (**Me → Manage account → Delete account**). Deletion calls our `delete_my_account` function, which removes your **auth user**; related rows in our database are removed through **cascade deletes** (profile, habits, check-ins, follows, tokens, etc.).

**Caveats we are honest about:**

- **Storage files** (proof photos, avatars) may take additional time to purge from backups or CDN caches.
- **Payment records** with Razorpay may be retained by Razorpay under their policy.
- **Aggregated or de-identified** data that cannot reasonably identify you may be retained.

### Data export

You can request a copy of your data in the app via **Export my data**, which returns a JSON bundle of your profile, habits, check-ins, streaks, and follow relationships.

## 9. Security

We use industry-standard measures appropriate to the service, including:

- **HTTPS** for API and storage access
- **Row Level Security (RLS)** on database tables so clients only access permitted rows
- **Private proof storage** with signed, time-limited URLs for authorized viewers
- **Owner-only** access to push tokens and subscription rows from the client
- **OTP rate limiting** to reduce abuse

No system is 100% secure. You are responsible for keeping your phone and SMS messages secure. If you believe your account was compromised, contact us at [privacy@weekup.app](mailto:privacy@weekup.app).
